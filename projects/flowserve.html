Overview

Applications Engineering

As an Applications Engineering Co-op supporting a Flowserve Quick Response Center, I assist applications engineers with mechanical seal failure analysis, engineering documentation, industrial troubleshooting, and engineering automation.

My work combines hands-on mechanical inspection with analytical problem solving to help identify root causes of seal failures, support repair decisions, and improve internal engineering workflows.

01

Responsibility 01

Mechanical Seal Repair Evaluation

Mechanical seals arriving at the Quick Response Center are first documented before being disassembled for inspection. I prepare the initial repair evaluation, disassemble the seal, and inspect the condition of its critical components.

Components such as seal faces, sleeves, glands, O-rings, springs, and associated hardware are photographed and evaluated for wear, damage, deposits, corrosion, contamination, or abnormal contact patterns. These observations provide the physical evidence used during repair and failure analysis.

02

Responsibility 02

Seal Failure Analysis

Following inspection, I prepare seal failure analysis reports by combining physical evidence from the seal with operating information collected from the customer site and historical equipment records.

Root-cause investigations may involve reviewing field notes from operators and technicians, maintenance history, pump curves, service conditions, seal piping plans, and the mechanical condition of the returned seal. The objective is to determine whether damage resulted from normal wear or from an external condition causing premature failure.

Depending on the available evidence, potential causes may include dry running, inadequate flush conditions, process contamination, excessive vibration, installation issues, or operating the pump outside its intended range.

04

Engineering Automation

Python BOM Automation

A customer spare-kit project required engineers to manually compare multiple SAP bill-of-material databases to identify compatible kits for a large population of pumps. The process involved searching separate kit, driven-end, non-driven-end, and pump datasets before matching the resulting assemblies to individual pump records.

To automate this workflow, I developed a Python program using pandas that imports the separate Excel datasets, removes irrelevant records, standardizes the required columns, and compares component and bill-of-material identifiers across the different databases.

The matching results are then connected to the corresponding pump records and organized into a structured output suitable for upload into the company engineering database. This replaces a repetitive manual comparison process with a reusable data-processing workflow.
