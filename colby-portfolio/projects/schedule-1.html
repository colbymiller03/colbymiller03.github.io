Project Overview

Remotely operated LOX / ethanol rocket test stand developed by an independent student engineering team.

Schedule 1 is an independently funded student engineering project focused on developing a fully remotely operated LOX/ethanol static-fire test stand.

The project is intended to demonstrate that sophisticated liquid-rocket propulsion testing can be conducted safely, reliably, and repeatedly outside of a traditional university research laboratory.

The long-term objective is to perform every major phase of engine testing remotely from the Friends of Amateur Rocketry launch-control center. This includes valve actuation, regulator-pressure adjustment, system monitoring, propellant loading, ignition, and emergency shutdown.

My responsibilities focus on propulsion and fluids engineering, including engine-performance analysis, nozzle design, CFD validation, thermal and structural analysis, component sizing, pressure-loss calculations, P&ID development, and feed-system CAD.
