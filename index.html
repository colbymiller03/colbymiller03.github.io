About Me

I am a senior Mechanical Engineering student at California State University, Long Beach graduating in Fall 2026 with a passion for thermal-fluid systems and the analysis, design, and testing of those systems. I enjoy engineering problems that don't stop at the computer—taking an idea from initial design and simulation through manufacturing, assembly, and real-world validation.

My interest in engineering started long before college while growing up in the small desert town of Ridgecrest, California. I spent much of my time racing desert motorcycles, and nearly every weekend was spent rebuilding engines, replacing worn components, troubleshooting failures, and getting bikes ready for the next race.

After transferring to CSULB, I focused on applying those skills to real engineering projects. As Assistant Fluids Lead for the Beach Launch Team, I helped manufacture, assemble, and test a liquid rocket propulsion system. I currently work as an Applications Engineering Co-op at Flowserve, where I support mechanical seal failure analysis and develop Python tools to automate engineering workflows. Outside of work, I serve as Fluids Lead for Schedule 1, an independently funded student rocket project developing a reusable LOX/ethanol test stand.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy backpacking, fishing, camping, training in the gym, and spending time with friends and family. When time allows, I also look forward to getting back into desert motorcycle racing—the hobby that first inspired me to pursue mechanical engineering.
